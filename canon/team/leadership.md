# Leadership Rules

> Team: Leadership. Lead: `<CEO / co-CEOs>` (file in [`people/`](../../people/)).
> Takes effect under the company rules. If in conflict with [`company-rules.md`](../company-rules.md), the company rules win.
> Only the CEO(s) may edit this file.
>
> **Example team file.** Replace the bracketed bits with your reality; the structure is the point.

---

## What Leadership owns

- Company strategy, annual themes, and cross-team prioritization.
- Hiring, org design, and team-lead appointments.
- Governance: who can edit canon, decision authority, escalation of last resort.
- Investor, board, and fundraising matters (these stay out of team-facing signals — see exclusions below).

## Anti-scope — what Leadership does NOT own

- Day-to-day execution inside a team (that's the team lead's call).
- Routine deal terms within the approved threshold (Sales owns these).

## Hard rules

1. **Canon changes go through a PR, owner-approved.** No direct push to `canon/**`, founders included.
2. **Investor / M&A / equity / individual-performance content never enters a team-facing signal or recap.** It lives in leadership-only docs.
3. **Strategy decisions land as commits on canon files**, not as folklore. If a decision changes how agents behave, it changes a file.

## What Leadership's signals and recaps must never contain

- Compensation, equity, or incentive-plan details for any individual.
- Investor / board / M&A discussions.
- Internal pricing strategy.
- Individual performance reviews.

## Escalation path

- Cross-team conflict with no clear owner → CEO(s).
- Legal / contract / compliance concern → CEO(s).
- Anything that would change canon → a canon PR.
