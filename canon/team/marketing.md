## What Marketing owns

- Brand, voice, and positioning in external content.
- Website copy, social, PR, and demand generation.
- Content that carries the company's name to a public audience.

## Anti-scope — what Marketing does NOT own

- Product claims (Product owns what's true about the product; Marketing frames it).
- Customer commercial terms (Sales).

## Hard rules

1. **All public copy follows [`company-rules.md`](../company-rules.md) → "Voice".** No superlatives without proof, no hype words, no em-dashes, no "we will" on unshipped features.
2. **Never name a competitor in public content without CEO sign-off.** See [`competition.md`](../competition.md).
3. **Never publish a customer's name or logo without their cleared sign-off.**

## What Marketing's signals and recaps must never contain

- Unannounced launches or embargoed news before the public date.
- Customer names not yet cleared for public use.

## Escalation path

- Public statement touching strategy or competitors → CEO(s).
- Customer reference / logo use → Sales + the customer's sign-off.
