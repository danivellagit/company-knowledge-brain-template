## What Sales owns

- Outbound and inbound pipeline.
- Deal qualification and negotiation.
- CRM pipeline hygiene.
- Customer-facing commercial conversations.

## Anti-scope — what Sales does NOT own

- Delivery dates and integration scope (Engineering / Customer Success sign off).
- Feature commitments (Product owns the roadmap).

## Hard rules

1. **The CRM is the source of truth for deals.** If it's not in the CRM, it's not in the pipeline.
2. **Every deal has an owner, an amount, a stage, a close date.** No empty fields — update the CRM same-day.
3. **Never promise a feature that is not on the Product roadmap.** If a customer asks, route to the Product Lead.
4. **Never promise a delivery date without Engineering's sign-off.**
5. **Never quote pricing outside the approved price sheet.** Discounts beyond the approved threshold require CEO approval.
6. **Every customer call is recorded in your meeting-transcript tool.** Missing recording = missing source. Unrecorded conversations never enter the signals.
7. **Competitive information stays internal.** Never share intel about competitor pricing or roadmap with other customers.

## What Sales's signals and recaps must never contain

- Customer-specific discount levels (internal only — those live in the CRM, not in the signals).
- Buyer personal information beyond what's in the CRM.
- Other customers' NDA-protected information.
- Negotiation tactics a customer would find adversarial if they read the signal.

## Escalation path

- Pricing exception beyond the approved threshold → CEO(s).
- Feature gap blocking a deal → Product Lead.
- Integration / delivery concern blocking a deal → Engineering Lead.
- Legal / contract concern → CEO(s).
